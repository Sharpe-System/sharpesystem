      Document checklist

      Start with what you already have.

      • Policy number (or a photo of the card)
      • The letter/email you received (full page, not cropped)
      • Photos of damage or the issue (wide + close)
      • Receipts and estimates (even if incomplete)
      • Claim number and adjuster contact (if you have it)

      Email help

      Short. Calm. Firm.

      Subject: Claim follow-up — request for next step

      Hello, I’m following up on my claim. Please confirm what you need from me next and the deadline to provide it. If you already requested documents, please list them again in one place. Thank you.

      Call plan

      Do not hang up without clarity.

      • “What is the next step on your side?”
      • “What is the next step on my side?”
      • “What documents are missing, exactly?”
      • “What is the deadline?”
      • “When should I expect an update if I send this today?”
      • Write down: date, time, name, and what they promised.

      Inspection prep

      Document damage like a normal person, but thoroughly.

      • Take wide shots (context), then close shots (detail).
      • Include one photo with a common object for scale (coin, tape measure, shoe).
      • Do not repair or throw away damaged items until you know it’s okay.
      • If water is involved, take photos daily as it changes.

      Advocate summary

      One page. No drama. Just facts.

      - Claim type: (auto / property / medical / life)
      - Claim number: (if known)
      - What happened: (2–4 sentences)
      - What the insurer said: (quote or attach)
      - What’s due next: (documents / deadline)
      - What you need: (approval / payment / reconsideration / explanation)

      Follow-up schedule

      Stay ahead without obsessing.

      • Day 0: send documents (keep proof)
      • Day 3: polite follow-up if no confirmation
      • Day 7: request status + next step
      • Every 7 days after: one clean check-in
