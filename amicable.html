Let’s resolve this amicably

A structured, low-friction way to communicate early—before conflict hardens into filings. Informational only. Not legal advice.

Principles (what makes this work)

  • One topic at a time, in writing
  • Concrete proposals, not grievances
  • Time-boxed responses
  • Document agreements immediately

A simple 4-step structure

  1. Define the topic (one sentence)
  2. State your proposal (specific, testable)
  3. List constraints (work schedule, school, travel, finances)
  4. Offer two alternatives (so the other person can say “yes” to something)

Template you can copy

Use this format to keep communication productive:

Topic: [e.g., schedule for next 30 days]

Proposal: [specific proposal]

Constraints: [hard limits and non-negotiables]

Alternatives: (A) […] (B) […]

Response requested by: [date/time]
