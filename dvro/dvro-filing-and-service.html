Filing, Service, and Hearing Preparation

Once information and requests are organized, the court process follows a steady sequence. Knowing that sequence reduces uncertainty and helps maintain momentum during a difficult period.

Typical DVRO sequence
  1. Prepare request forms and incident declaration
  2. File with Orange County family court
  3. Judge review for temporary orders and hearing date
  4. Serve filed papers and any temporary orders
  5. File proof of service before the hearing

Hearing preparation usually includes a clean incident timeline, organized exhibits, and printed copies. Completion means the packet is filed and service steps are underway.
