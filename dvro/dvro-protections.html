Requested Protections

Restraining orders work through clarity. Each requested term becomes a boundary the court can enforce. This step organizes those boundaries so the order reflects the real risks and living situation involved.

Requests are usually strongest when they directly match the incidents described.

Common DVRO protections
  • No contact (calls, texts, online, third-party)
  • Stay-away distance from home, work, school, vehicles
  • Move-out order for shared residence
  • No harassment or threats
  • Firearms restrictions
Child-related protections (if applicable)
  • Temporary custody and visitation terms
  • Exchange safety logistics
  • Limited or supervised contact provisions
