Relationship and Incident Summary

Restraining order papers are often strongest when events are laid out calmly and chronologically. This step gathers the relationship category and the incidents in a way the court can read quickly without losing the reality of what occurred.

Precision matters more than volume. Short factual descriptions are easier for courts to rely on than broad narrative.

Relationship category
  • Spouse or former spouse
  • Dating relationship
  • Co-parent
  • Family member
  • Household member
Incident timeline
  • Date or approximate timeframe
  • Location
  • What happened (conduct)
  • Impact (fear, injury, disruption)

If evidence exists, listing types is enough at this stage: messages, photos, recordings, witness names, medical or police records.
