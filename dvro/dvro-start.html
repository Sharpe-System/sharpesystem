Domestic Violence Restraining Order — Starting Point

When safety and stability have been disrupted by someone close, the court can issue a Domestic Violence Restraining Order (DVRO) to create clear legal distance and enforceable boundaries. This process often begins during already overwhelming circumstances. The goal here is quiet structure: what the court needs, in what order, without guesswork.

This section prepares information for Orange County family court DVRO forms and procedure. It does not determine outcomes and does not replace court instructions.

Relationships that commonly qualify
  • Spouse or former spouse
  • Dating partner or former dating partner
  • Co-parent (shared child)
  • Close family member
  • Household member (current or former)

Safety note: If there is immediate danger, call 911.
