Gather the Right Documents First

DMV staff can only issue an ID card when the documents meet specific standards. Bringing the right paperwork up front reduces delays, repeat visits, and unnecessary stress.

Original or certified documents are commonly required. Copies may not be accepted. Name mismatches often require extra support documents.

Proof of identity (examples)
  • U.S. passport
  • U.S. birth certificate
  • Permanent resident card
  • Employment authorization document
  • Other DMV-accepted identity record
Proof of California residency (examples)
  • Utility bill or service statement
  • Rental/lease document or housing letter
  • School, medical, or insurance record
  • Government correspondence
  • Other DMV-accepted residency record

Common friction points: expired documents, different last names across records, missing birth certificate, unstable housing mail. If any of these apply, the next page includes a practical appointment plan.
